The main cause of road accidents and aggravated traffic congestion is the violation and deliberate violation of traffic rules by drivers. The efficiency of traffic enforcers in arresting these traffic violators has become a problem. As there are no current policies or technology in place that can invariably reduce, if not eliminate, the rate of traffic violations, undisciplined drivers tend to ignore the rules. This research background literature discusses the various image processing techniques that have been developed or enhanced to identify and address traffic violations, with a focus on real-time systems and plate character recognition technologies. By gaining a deeper understanding of these techniques and their applications, it is hoped that societies can reduce the impact of traffic violations and improve overall road safety.The consequences of traffic violations are both immediate and far-reaching, impacting not only the violators themselves but also other road users and the community at large. Traffic violations often lead to accidents, property damage, injuries, and fatalities, as well as increased congestion and delays. These issues result in considerable economic costs, environmental impacts, and diminished quality of life for individuals living in affected areas [1].Given the severity of these consequences, there is a pressing need for effective solutions to identify and address traffic violations. Efficient traffic enforcement systems can help ensure that drivers adhere to traffic rules, thus reducing the likelihood of accidents and congestion. By identifying and apprehending traffic violators, these systems can serve as a deterrent to other potential offenders, promoting a culture of road safety and compliance. Recent research and trends have focused on developing or enhancing various image processing techniques, which can help identify and address traffic violations. These techniques aim to analyze the types of violations that occur on the road and [2]detect infractions in real-time. Examples of applications include object and image recognition, which can be employed to detect vehicles violating traffic rules or to [3]identify specific license plate information [2] [4].One of the most commonly used methods for plate recognition is Optical Character Recognition (OCR). OCR technology is capable of converting printed or handwritten text into machine-readable text, allowing for the efficient identification and processing of license plate information. This technology has become instrumental in detecting and apprehending traffic violators, enabling enforcers to take prompt action against offenders [4]
